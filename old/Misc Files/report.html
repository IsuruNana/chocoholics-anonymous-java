COHESION AND COUPLING ANALYSIS

Cohesion and Coupling

  1. Member and Provider extend the User class because they share fields and operations. (Functional Cohesion)
  2. The Member and Provider interact with Operator seperately. (Low Coupling)
  3. Provider directory interacts with Provider, Member Records and Member Report. (Data Coupling)

Why the deisgn is good?

None of the classes have unrelated actions within their implementation. This avoids coincidental cohesion.
The abstract classes User, Records and Report are easily reusable as they define a very generalized class that can be extended efficiently.
The Records classes perform only one operation (printDetails()) and this promotes functional cohesion.
The Timer class performs weekly updates to its communicating classes and as a result performs Temporal Cohesion.
Because of its ability to control other classes and send control over to them it also has control coupling.
Both aspects promote a strong structure. The BillChocAn and Provider classes are interact with each other but
perform operations that take in less than or equal to 1 parameter.
Changes in BillChocAn or Provider should not affect their individual functionality. This way they are strongly data coupled.
In summary our design has classes that have high cohesion and low coupling of modules.
